Adobo

Philippine adobo is a popular Filipino dish and cooking process in Philippine cuisine of, in its base form, meat, seafood, or vegetables first browned in oil, and then marinated and simmered vinegar, salt and/or soy sauce, and garlic. It has occasionally been considered the unofficial national dish in the Philippines

    Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 (3 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, quartered and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • ⅔ cup low sodium soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf

    Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Cook chicken pieces until golden brown, 2 to 3 minutes per side.
  3. Transfer chicken to a plate and set aside.
  4. Add onion and garlic to the skillet; cook until softened and brown, about 6 minutes.
  5. Pour in soy sauce and vinegar and season with garlic powder, black pepper, and bay leaf.
  6. Return chicken to pan, increase heat to high, and bring to a boil.
  7. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer
  8. until chicken is tender and cooked through, 35 to 40 minutes.
